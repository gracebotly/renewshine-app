Your DC landlord isn't going to call after the walkthrough and say "great job." They're looking for reasons to keep your deposit. That's not cynicism — that's how the math works. A security deposit in DC is capped at one month's rent (DC Code § 42-3261), and landlords have 45 days to return it. If you leave the oven dirty and the baseboards dusty, they'll point at that. Your job is to give them nothing to point at.

This checklist covers what gets inspected, what gets missed, and what you can skip.

## What does a move-out clean include that a regular clean doesn't?

Move-out cleaning goes further than standard maintenance cleaning. Landlords expect the unit returned close to move-in condition, which means areas that rarely get touched during regular cleaning have to be done.

The additions that separate a move-out clean from a standard clean:

- Inside all kitchen cabinets and drawers (crumbs, spills, liner residue)
- Inside the oven (baked-on grease — this is the most commonly missed item)
- Inside the refrigerator (drawers, shelves, door seals, rubber gasket)
- Inside bathroom cabinets
- Tops of cabinets (dust accumulation, often forgotten)
- Spot-cleaned walls, especially in the kitchen and bathroom
- Vacuum edges of carpet along baseboards

If your cleaning service doesn't include all of those, clarify before you book. "Deep clean" doesn't automatically mean move-out clean.

## Room-by-room checklist

Use this before your walkthrough. Check each item yourself after the cleaning is done.

### Kitchen

- [ ] Counters wiped down, including under appliances
- [ ] Sink scrubbed, including faucet base and drain
- [ ] Cabinet fronts wiped
- [ ] Inside all cabinets and drawers — crumbs, stains, spills
- [ ] Tops of cabinets (dust and grease)
- [ ] Stovetop, burner grates, and knob panels
- [ ] Inside oven — baked-on grease and residue
- [ ] Oven racks and door glass
- [ ] Inside microwave — ceiling, walls, turntable
- [ ] Exterior of fridge, stove, dishwasher
- [ ] Inside refrigerator — all drawers, shelves, door seals
- [ ] Vent hood exterior and filter
- [ ] Floors swept and mopped, including under appliances if accessible
- [ ] Walls near stove for grease splatter

### Bathrooms

- [ ] Toilet — bowl, tank, base, hinges, behind
- [ ] Sink and faucet — no soap scum, no hard water stains
- [ ] Mirror (streak-free)
- [ ] Inside vanity cabinet and drawers
- [ ] Shower or tub — tiles, grout lines, caulk, drain
- [ ] Shower door or curtain rod
- [ ] Floor including corners and grout
- [ ] Baseboards
- [ ] Light fixtures and exhaust fan cover
- [ ] Walls for soap scum and water stains

### Bedrooms

- [ ] All surfaces dusted — shelves, windowsills, furniture
- [ ] Inside closets and shelves — dust, debris, items left behind
- [ ] Ceiling fan blades
- [ ] Light switches and outlet covers
- [ ] Carpet vacuumed, including edges along baseboards
- [ ] Hard floors swept and mopped
- [ ] Behind and under accessible furniture (if leaving items)
- [ ] Windows wiped inside

### Living areas

- [ ] All surfaces dusted
- [ ] Blinds or window coverings — dust and wipe
- [ ] Windowsills — dust, grime, dead bugs
- [ ] Baseboards throughout
- [ ] Light fixtures
- [ ] Ceiling fans if present
- [ ] Floors vacuumed and mopped
- [ ] Walls for scuffs (spot clean — you don't need to repaint for normal wear)

### Throughout

- [ ] All light switches and outlet covers wiped
- [ ] Door handles and plates wiped
- [ ] Doors themselves wiped (top edge, both sides)
- [ ] Vents dusted within reach
- [ ] Baseboards in every room
- [ ] Window tracks cleaned

## What's protected as normal wear and tear?

Landlords across DC, Virginia, and Maryland cannot deduct for normal wear and tear — only for actual damage or serious neglect. Here's the difference:

| Normal wear and tear (not deductible) | Damage (deductible) |
|---|---|
| Minor wall scuffs from furniture | Holes punched in drywall |
| Faded paint from sunlight | Paint heavily stained or marked |
| Worn carpet from foot traffic | Carpet stains or burns |
| Small nail holes from hanging pictures | Large anchor holes left unfilled |
| Dusty blinds | Broken blind slats |
| Worn finish on hardwood floors | Deep scratches from dragging furniture |

In DC specifically, the Office of the Tenant Advocate actively helps tenants dispute improper deductions. If you document the condition at move-out with photos and timestamps, you have a strong position.

## The three things landlords check first

Based on what causes most deposit disputes in the DMV:

**1. Inside the oven.** This is the single most commonly failed item in self-done move-out cleans. Baked-on grease takes specific cleaners and time. Landlords open the oven door within the first two minutes of any walkthrough.

**2. Bathroom grout.** Mold and mildew in shower grout lines is a standard deduction item. It needs actual grout cleaner and a brush, not just a spray-and-wipe.

**3. Baseboards behind furniture.** Dust accumulation behind couches and beds that were never moved. This is where people get caught on "general dirtiness" deductions.

## DC vs. Virginia vs. Maryland deposit rules

The rules vary by where you're renting. Here's what matters for move-out:

| | Washington DC | Virginia | Maryland |
|---|---|---|---|
| Max deposit | 1 month's rent | 2 months' rent | 1 month's rent (leases after Oct 1, 2024) |
| Return deadline | 45 days | 45 days | 45 days |
| Penalty for late return | 3x the deposit amount | Full deposit + attorney fees | Full deposit + damages |
| Receipts required for deductions | Yes | Yes (over $125) | Yes |
| Interest on deposit | Yes (annually) | No | Yes (1.5%/yr) |

DC tenants have the most to lose from deposit deductions — deposits are capped at one month's rent, so even small deductions hurt proportionally. Get the cleaning right.

### FAQ

#### How much does move-out cleaning cost in Washington DC?

Most DC apartments and homes range from $350–$600 for a professional move-out clean depending on size and condition. #### Does move-out cleaning include inside the oven and fridge?

A proper move-out clean always includes inside the oven and inside the refrigerator. These are the two items landlords check first. If your cleaning service doesn't include them as standard, ask explicitly before you book.

#### How far in advance should I book move-out cleaning in DC?

Book at least 3–5 days before your move-out date. The day before your walkthrough is the ideal timing — close enough that the space stays clean, late enough to fit after your belongings are out.

#### What can a DC landlord legally deduct from my security deposit?

DC landlords can deduct for damage beyond normal wear and tear, unpaid rent, and serious cleaning issues. They cannot deduct for minor wall scuffs, faded paint, or worn carpet. DC caps deposits at one month's rent (DC Code § 42-3261), and failure to return it within 45 days entitles tenants to 3x the deposit amount.

#### Can I do the move-out cleaning myself?

You can, but the most commonly failed items — inside the oven, grout lines, baseboards behind furniture — are the ones that take the longest and are easiest to miss when you're also managing a move. A professional clean takes these off your list and gives you documentation that the work was done.
