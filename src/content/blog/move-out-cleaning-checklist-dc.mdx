  - question: "Does move-out cleaning include inside the oven and fridge?"
    answer: "A proper move-out clean always includes inside the oven and inside the refrigerator. These are the two items landlords check first. If your cleaning service doesn't include them as standard, ask explicitly — otherwise you may fail the inspection on items you thought were covered."
  - question: "How far in advance should I book move-out cleaning in DC?"
    answer: "Book at least 3–5 days before your move-out date. The day before your walkthrough is the ideal timing — close enough that the space stays clean, late enough to fit after your belongings are out."
  - question: "What can a DC landlord legally deduct from my security deposit?"
    answer: "DC landlords can deduct for damage beyond normal wear and tear, unpaid rent, and serious cleaning issues. They cannot deduct for minor wall scuffs, faded paint, or worn carpet from regular use. DC caps deposits at one month's rent (DC Code § 42-3261), and failure to return it within 45 days entitles tenants to 3x the deposit amount."
  - question: "Can I do the move-out cleaning myself?"
    answer: "You can, but the most commonly failed items — inside the oven, grout lines, baseboards behind furniture, and window sills — are the ones that take the longest and are easiest to miss when you're also managing a move. A professional clean takes these off your list and gives you documentation that the work was done."
