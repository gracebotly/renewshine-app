faq:
  - question: "How much does house cleaning cost in Washington DC?"
    answer: "Standard cleaning in DC typically runs $200–$380 for a 2–3 bedroom home. Deep cleaning starts at $400 and runs $500–$700 for larger homes. Move-out cleaning is $350–$700+ depending on the property size and condition. Final prices are confirmed after reviewing the space — not quoted by phone based on bedroom count alone."
  - question: "Why do cleaning service prices vary so much?"
    answer: "Bedroom count is a rough proxy, not an accurate measure of scope. A 2BR condo in Dupont Circle is a very different job than a 2BR townhouse in Old Town with three floors and a basement. Condition, layout, add-ons, and how recently the home was professionally cleaned all affect the actual price."
  - question: "Is it cheaper to hire a cleaning service or an individual cleaner?"
    answer: "Individual cleaners typically charge $20–$35 per hour and may clean for 2–4 hours depending on the home. A service charging $200–$280 for the same job often includes insurance, supplies, and a team of two. The hourly rate comparison looks cheaper with an individual, but the total and the risk profile are different."
  - question: "How much does recurring cleaning cost in DC?"
    answer: "Recurring clients typically pay 10–20% less than one-time rates. A standard clean that costs $240 for a one-time booking might run $195–$215 bi-weekly. Frequency discounts are usually applied manually by the service, not automatically — ask upfront."
  - question: "Do cleaning services in DC bring their own supplies?"
    answer: "Most professional services bring their own supplies. Independent cleaners sometimes don't — confirm before booking. If you have specific product preferences (unscented, eco-friendly, a brand you trust), ask whether they'll accommodate or bring your preferred supplies."
