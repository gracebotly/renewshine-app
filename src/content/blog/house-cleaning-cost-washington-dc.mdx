Most cleaning service websites show a starting price and nothing else. You call, get a quote, and then the cleaner arrives and says something costs extra. That's how the industry has worked forever.

Here's what cleaning actually costs in the DC metro area in 2026 — real ranges, what drives the variation, and how to get a price that doesn't change at the door.

## What does standard cleaning cost in Washington DC?

Standard cleaning is maintenance cleaning — the ongoing service for a home that's regularly kept up.

**Typical price ranges in the DC area:**

| Home size | Estimated range |
|---|---|
| Studio or 1BR/1BA | $150–$200 |
| 2BR/1BA | $200–$250 |
| 2BR/2BA | $240–$300 |
| 3BR/2BA | $300–$380 |
| 4BR/3BA | $380–$480 |
| Large homes (5BR+) | $480+ (quoted per property) |

These are estimates for homes in reasonable condition with standard layouts. A 3BR colonial in Bethesda on a large lot with multiple living areas will be closer to the top of the range than a 3BR condo in Arlington with a single-floor layout.

Add-ons like laundry ($43), dishes ($25), linen changes ($15/bed), or interior windows ($20) add to the base price.

## What does deep cleaning cost?

Deep cleaning covers everything in a standard clean plus inside the oven, inside the refrigerator, grease removal, hard water stain treatment, under and behind accessible furniture, vents, cobwebs, and wet-wiped baseboards and light switches.

**Typical ranges:**

| Home size | Estimated range |
|---|---|
| 1BR/1BA | $400 |
| 2BR/1BA | $400 |
| 2BR/2BA | $400–$480 |
| 3BR/2BA | $490–$580 |
| 4BR/3BA | $580–$700 |
| Large homes | $700+ |

The floor is $400 for most smaller homes — that's what the work actually costs to do properly. Services quoting deep cleans for $200 are either not including the oven and fridge, or they're planning to make up the difference at the door.

## What does move-out cleaning cost?

Move-out cleaning includes everything in a deep clean plus inside all cabinets, cupboards, and closets, tops of cabinets, spot-cleaned walls in kitchen and bathrooms, and edge vacuuming of carpet.

Move-out cleaning is always quoted after reviewing the property. Every vacant unit is different — condition, how recently it was professionally cleaned, and what the previous tenant left behind all affect the scope significantly.

**Rough starting points:**
- 1BR/1BA apartment: $350–$450
- 2BR/2BA condo: $450–$580
- 3BR/2BA townhouse: $550–$700
- 4BR+ home: $700+

These are starting points, not locked prices. A unit in excellent condition from a careful tenant is a very different job than one left by a tenant who never cleaned the oven.

## What affects price the most?

**Condition.** A home that hasn't been professionally cleaned in 12 months takes significantly more time than one cleaned monthly. Heavy grease buildup, soap scum, hard water staining, and embedded pet hair all add time.

**Layout complexity.** Three-floor townhouses, homes with finished basements, properties with many rooms or irregular layouts take longer than single-floor condos with similar bedroom counts.

**Add-on services.** Interior windows, laundry, dishes, linen changes, organization — these add meaningfully. A $240 standard clean can become $340 once add-ons are factored in.

**Access logistics.** Buildings with elevator restrictions, limited parking, or strict move-in windows can affect scheduling — less so the price, but worth noting when booking.

## DC vs. Arlington vs. Bethesda vs. McLean

Prices don't vary dramatically by city within the DMV, but home types do — and home type affects scope.

A 4BR/3BA in McLean is often 4,000–6,000 sq ft with high-end finishes, multiple living areas, and detailed millwork. A 4BR/3BA in Silver Spring is often a colonial on a standard lot with a more typical layout. Same bedroom count, very different jobs. This is exactly why photo review matters more than a bedroom-based quote for larger properties.

The market price for cleaning services across DC, Arlington, Alexandria, Bethesda, and the Northern Virginia suburbs is reasonably consistent. Premium markets like McLean and Potomac don't charge more because the market expects it — they charge more because the jobs actually take longer.

### FAQ

#### How much does house cleaning cost in Washington DC?

Standard cleaning in DC typically runs $200–$380 for a 2–3 bedroom home. Deep cleaning starts at $400 and runs $500–$700 for larger homes. Move-out cleaning is $350–$700+ depending on size and condition. Final prices are confirmed after reviewing the space.

#### Why do cleaning service prices vary so much?

Bedroom count is a rough proxy, not an accurate measure of scope. Condition, layout, add-ons, and how recently the home was professionally cleaned all affect the actual price.

#### Is it cheaper to hire a cleaning service or an individual cleaner?

Individual cleaners typically charge $20–$35 per hour. A service charging $200–$280 for the same job often includes insurance, supplies, and a team of two. The hourly rate looks cheaper with an individual, but the total and the risk profile are different.

#### How much does recurring cleaning cost in DC?

Recurring clients typically pay 10–20% less than one-time rates. A $240 standard clean might run $195–$215 bi-weekly. Ask about frequency discounts upfront — they're not always applied automatically.

#### Do cleaning services in DC bring their own supplies?

Most professional services bring their own supplies. If you have specific product preferences, ask before booking whether they'll accommodate.
