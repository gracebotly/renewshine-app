Most people who book a cleaning service aren't sure which type they need. Standard and deep cleaning sound like obvious categories until you're actually looking at the checklist — and then the line gets blurry.

Here's the simple way to think about it: standard cleaning is maintenance. Deep cleaning is a reset.

## What does standard cleaning include?

Standard cleaning covers the things that need regular attention — the surfaces and areas you actually use every week.

**Kitchen:** Counter surfaces, sink, cabinet fronts, exterior of oven and fridge, microwave inside and out, stovetop and knobs, floors swept and mopped.

**Bathrooms:** Sink and counter, mirror, cabinet exteriors, toilet, shower and tub, floors swept and mopped.

**Living areas and bedrooms:** Dust all surfaces and furniture, dust window sills and frames, vacuum floors and upholstery, mop hard floors, make beds with existing linens.

**Highs and lows:** Dust light fixtures, ceiling fans, blinds, and baseboards.

That's a thorough clean of everything visible and frequently touched. For a home that's kept up between visits, standard cleaning is all you need.

## What does deep cleaning add?

Deep cleaning goes after the things that don't get addressed in regular maintenance — the grease, buildup, and hard-to-reach areas that accumulate over months.

**Kitchen additions:**
- Inside the oven (baked-on grease, residue — included at no extra charge)
- Inside the refrigerator (drawers, shelves, door seals — included at no extra charge)
- Grease buildup removal (vent hood, backsplash, top of fridge)
- Wipe accessible cabinet tops

**Bathroom additions:**
- Hard water stains, lime scale, rust, and soap scum removal
- Mold and mildew treatment in grout and caulk lines

**Throughout:**
- Remove and replace items on surfaces to clean underneath
- Under and behind accessible furniture
- Vents dusted within reach
- Cobwebs removed from corners
- Doors and baseboards wet-wiped (not just dusted)
- Light switches and outlet covers wet-wiped

That last category — light switches, outlet covers, door handles, vents — is what separates a deep clean from a thorough regular clean. These surfaces accumulate a kind of greasy grime that a standard dust doesn't touch.

## When is standard cleaning enough?

Standard cleaning is the right call when your home is regularly maintained and you're booking a service to keep it that way.

Specifically, standard cleaning works well if:
- You clean yourself between professional visits
- You've had a professional clean within the last 1–2 months
- There's no grease buildup, hard water staining, or mold
- You're maintaining — not resetting

For recurring clients on a bi-weekly or monthly schedule, standard cleaning is almost always the right ongoing service. The deep clean is the starting point, not the regular cadence.

## When do you need a deep clean?

Several situations call for a deep clean specifically:

**First-time professional clean.** If your home hasn't had a professional service recently, there's likely buildup that a standard clean won't fully address. A deep clean creates the baseline that makes every subsequent standard clean faster and more thorough.

**Moving in.** Even if the previous tenant cleaned, professional move-out cleans vary in quality. A deep clean on arrival gives you a home you know is actually clean — grout, inside appliances, behind things.

**Moving out.** A deep clean is the right level of service for vacant properties, especially if you need to pass a landlord inspection. (Move-in/Move-out cleaning is technically a separate tier that goes further than Deep Clean — it adds inside all cabinets and closets, but the depth of work is similar.)

**Seasonal reset.** Once or twice a year, most homes benefit from going back to a deep clean baseline — even if you've had regular standard cleanings in between.

**After renovation or construction.** Fine dust from drywall and construction gets into vents, surfaces, and corners that a standard clean won't reach. Deep cleaning after renovation work is essentially a specialized service in itself.

**Before selling your home.** Buyers notice the oven, the fridge, the grout lines. A deep clean before listing makes those things invisible.
