faq:
  - question: "What's the difference between a deep clean and a standard clean?"
    answer: "Standard cleaning covers regular maintenance — surfaces, floors, kitchen exterior, bathrooms, dusting. Deep cleaning goes further: inside the oven and refrigerator, grease removal, hard water stains, under and behind accessible furniture, vents, cobwebs, and wet-wiped baseboards and light switches. It's recommended for first-time clients and homes that haven't had a professional clean recently."
  - question: "How much does deep cleaning cost in Washington DC?"
    answer: "Most DC homes range from $400–$600+ for a deep clean depending on size and condition. A 2BR/2BA in good condition typically runs $400–$480. Larger homes or those with heavy buildup will be higher. RenewShine confirms your exact price after reviewing your photos — no guessing by bedroom count alone."
  - question: "How often should you get a deep clean?"
    answer: "Most households benefit from a deep clean once or twice a year, or whenever a significant life event happens — moving in, moving out, after a renovation, before selling. If you have a recurring standard clean, an annual deep clean in spring or fall keeps things at a baseline the weekly or monthly cleans can maintain."
  - question: "Does deep cleaning include inside the oven and fridge?"
    answer: "Yes — at RenewShine, inside the oven and inside the refrigerator are standard inclusions on every Deep Clean, not add-ons. Most services treat these as extras. We include them because they're what clients actually expect when they hear 'deep clean.'"
  - question: "Should first-time clients get a deep clean?"
    answer: "For most first-time clients, yes. A deep clean establishes a baseline that makes every subsequent standard clean faster and more thorough. If your home hasn't had a professional clean in more than a few months, there's likely buildup in places a standard clean won't fully address."
